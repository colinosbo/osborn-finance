# Osborn Finance — Reports & Spending Insights: Requirements

**Status:** Draft / requirements
**Owner:** Web + API
**Builds on:** existing analytics engine (`server/src/analytics.ts` — `summarize` + `advise`), preferences layer (`web/src/prefs.ts`)
**Depends on (later):** email provider, SMS provider, scheduled-task runner, PDF renderer
**Last updated:** 2026-06-12

---

## 1. Overview & purpose

Reports turn a user's raw transaction history into a periodic, human-readable story about their money. Where the Dashboard answers "what's happening right now," a Report answers "how did this **week / month / half-year / year** actually go, and what should I do about it?"

Each report is a self-contained, shareable summary covering a fixed time window. It combines hard numbers (income, spend, net, savings rate, category and merchant breakdowns, month-over-month trend) with **plain-language insights** (the advisor tips already generated by `advise()`), and frames them against the **previous comparable period** so the user sees direction, not just totals. A report is something a user can open in the app, receive in their inbox on a schedule, get pinged about by text, and download as a PDF or CSV to keep or share with a partner/accountant.

The product goal is behavioral: a good report makes the user feel informed and slightly accountable, surfaces one or two concrete actions, and celebrates progress (e.g., "your savings rate climbed from 9% to 14% this month"). It must never feel like a punishing audit; tone is supportive and specific.

There are four report cadences:

- **Weekly** — a quick pulse. The last 7 days vs. the prior 7. Designed for a Monday-morning glance and a short SMS/email.
- **Monthly** — the core report. A full calendar month vs. the prior month, with category detail and 2–3 insights.
- **6-month** — a trend report. Two quarters of momentum: category drift, recurring-cost creep, savings-rate trajectory, seasonality.
- **Year in review** — the flagship. A celebratory, comprehensive annual recap ("your money, this year") with headline stats, biggest categories, biggest single purchases, total saved, subscriptions audited, and a narrative summary. Heavier visual treatment, shareable.

## 2. Functional requirements

### 2.1 Report periods & windows

- **FR-1** The system shall generate reports for four period types: `weekly`, `monthly`, `six_month`, `year_in_review`.
- **FR-2** Period boundaries shall be well-defined and consistent:
  - Weekly: a 7-day window (configurable week-start; default Monday) ending on the report date.
  - Monthly: a calendar month (1st → last day).
  - Six-month: a rolling 182-day window, or two calendar quarters (decide in design; default rolling 6 calendar months).
  - Year in review: a calendar year, or a rolling trailing 365 days for mid-year generation.
- **FR-3** Each report shall compute against the user's transactions in that window using the existing `summarize(tx, days, latest)` engine, and generate insights with `advise(tx, days, latest)`. Reports reuse these — they do not introduce a second, divergent calculation of the same numbers.
- **FR-4** Every report shall include a **comparison to the immediately preceding comparable period** (prior week/month/6-month/year), expressed as absolute and percentage deltas for: total spend, total income, net, savings rate, and each top category.
- **FR-5** Reports shall handle **partial / sparse data** gracefully: if a window has too few transactions or no income, the report still renders with clear "limited data" messaging rather than misleading zeros (mirroring the advisor's existing "No income detected" pinned tip).

### 2.2 Report content (insights)

Each report shall include, scaled to its cadence:

- **FR-6 Headline KPIs:** income, total spend, net cash flow, and savings rate for the period, each with its delta vs. the prior period.
- **FR-7 Spending by category:** ranked categories with totals, share-of-spend %, transaction counts, and change vs. prior period (use the category colors already defined in `web/src/api.ts COLORS`).
- **FR-8 Top merchants:** the period's largest merchants by spend (count + total).
- **FR-9 Trend:** month-by-month (or week-by-week for weekly) inflow/outflow series for charting; for 6-month and year, a clear sparkline/bar of net by month.
- **FR-10 Notable items:** largest single transactions, new merchants not seen in the prior period, and any one-off outliers (consistent with the `avgMonthly` outlier threshold logic).
- **FR-11 Recurring & subscriptions:** detected subscriptions and recurring charges, total monthly run-rate, and any *new* or *increased* recurring costs since last period.
- **FR-12 Insights / advice:** the prioritized advisor tips (pinned status + estimated `savePerMonth`), capped sensibly per cadence (e.g., weekly = 1–2, monthly = 3, year = up to 6) and written in the supportive tone already used by `advise()`.
- **FR-13 Year-in-review extras:** total spent for the year, total saved, biggest month, cheapest month, top 5 categories, top 5 merchants, single biggest purchase, number of transactions, subscriptions audited, and a generated 2–4 sentence narrative recap.
- **FR-14 Money formatting & dates** shall respect the user's preferences (`currency`, `dateFormat`, `timezone`) from `prefs.ts` everywhere they appear, in-app and in delivered email/SMS/PDF.

### 2.3 Generation & automation

- **FR-15 On-demand:** a user shall be able to generate/preview any report for any available period from the Reports page, instantly.
- **FR-16 Scheduled:** a user shall be able to subscribe to automatic recurring reports per cadence (e.g., "email me the weekly every Monday 7am", "monthly on the 1st"). Implemented via the scheduled-task runner; each fire generates the report for the just-closed period and delivers it through the chosen channels.
- **FR-17 Per-cadence, per-channel opt-in:** the user controls, independently, which cadences they receive and over which channels (in-app only, email, SMS). Reasonable defaults: monthly email on, others off.
- **FR-18 Idempotency:** a scheduled report for a given user + period + cadence shall not be generated or delivered twice (dedupe key), even if the job retries.
- **FR-19 Time zone correctness:** scheduled sends fire in the user's `timezone`, and period boundaries are computed in that zone.

### 2.4 Delivery channels

**In-app**
- **FR-20** Generated reports shall be viewable in-app and retained in a **report history** the user can browse and re-open.

**Email**
- **FR-21** Reports shall be deliverable as **responsive HTML email** with a plaintext fallback, containing the headline KPIs, top categories, 1–3 insights, and the period comparison — readable without leaving the inbox.
- **FR-22** The email shall include a deep link to the full report in-app and (optionally) a **PDF attachment** of the full report.
- **FR-23** Emails shall include required compliance elements: clear sender identity, the reason they're receiving it, and a one-click **unsubscribe / manage cadence** link. Unsubscribing from reports shall not affect transactional/security email.
- **FR-24** Email content shall contain **no full account numbers or access tokens**; only masked account references (e.g., `··3131`) and aggregate figures (consistent with finding L1 — no sensitive data in outbound content/logs).

**SMS / text (when available)**
- **FR-25** When the SMS capability is enabled, the user shall be able to opt in to receive a **short text digest** per chosen cadence (e.g., "Osborn Finance — last week: spent $612, saved 18%. Top: Dining $140. Full report: <link>").
- **FR-26** SMS shall be **strictly opt-in with explicit consent**, support **STOP/HELP** keyword handling, honor quiet hours, and comply with TCPA/carrier (10DLC) requirements. Phone numbers must be verified before any SMS is sent.
- **FR-27** SMS content shall be concise (target ≤ 1–2 segments), contain no sensitive financial identifiers, and always include a link to the full in-app report rather than detailed figures.
- **FR-28** The UI shall clearly mark SMS as "coming soon / unavailable" until the capability is live, and the opt-in control shall be present but disabled with explanatory copy, so the feature is discoverable now and flips on later without redesign.

### 2.5 Export

- **FR-29** Any report shall be exportable as **PDF** (full visual report) and **CSV** (the underlying tabular data: transactions in-period, category totals, merchant totals).
- **FR-30** Exports shall reflect the same period, numbers, and preferences as the on-screen report.

## 3. UI requirements

### 3.1 Reports page (`web/src/pages/Reports.tsx`)

- **UI-1 Period selector:** a prominent segmented control / tab row for **Weekly · Monthly · 6 Months · Year in Review**, plus a period picker to step back to previous periods (e.g., choose which month). Selecting a period regenerates the preview.
- **UI-2 Report header:** title, the resolved date range (in the user's date format), and the headline KPIs as a row of summary cards (reuse the existing `.cards`/`.card` pattern with entrance animation), each showing value + delta chip (green up / red down, with up/down arrow).
- **UI-3 Category breakdown:** a ranked list and/or donut/bar visual using the existing category `COLORS`, each row showing category, amount, share %, and Δ vs. prior period.
- **UI-4 Trend chart:** inflow/outflow (or net) over the period's sub-intervals (weeks for monthly, months for 6-month/year). Lightweight, on-brand, animated draw-in.
- **UI-5 Insights panel:** the advisor tips rendered with their icons and "save/month" badges (reuse the `.advrow` pattern), pinned items first.
- **UI-6 Notable & subscriptions:** compact panels for biggest purchases, new merchants, and recurring/subscriptions run-rate.
- **UI-7 Year-in-review mode:** a distinct, richer, more celebratory layout — large hero stat ("You spent $X this year"), highlight tiles, a narrative paragraph, and a "share" affordance. Visually clearly different from the routine weekly/monthly view.
- **UI-8 Actions per report:** **Download PDF**, **Download CSV**, **Email me this**, **Text me this** (disabled until SMS is live), and **Schedule…** (open delivery settings).
- **UI-9 Delivery settings:** a panel (or modal) where the user sets, per cadence, the channels and timing (e.g., weekly → email on, Monday 7am; SMS toggle present, disabled until available). Persists via preferences/API.
- **UI-10 Report history:** a list of previously generated/sent reports with date, cadence, channels delivered, and a re-open link.
- **UI-11 States:** polished **loading** (skeleton shimmer — `.skeleton` exists), **empty** ("not enough data for this period yet" with guidance), and **error** states.

### 3.2 Look & feel

- **UI-12** Reports shall match the existing design system (tokens, dark/light theme, `.panel`/`.card`/`.btn` vocabulary) and the new **motion layer**: staggered fade-up entrances, animated KPI/delta chips, chart draw-in, button press feedback. All animation must respect the **reduce-motion** preference and `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **UI-13** Fully **responsive** (single-column on mobile) and **accessible**: keyboard-navigable period selector and actions, sufficient color contrast, chart data also available as text/table, ARIA labels on interactive controls.
- **UI-14** Email and PDF shall carry consistent Osborn Finance branding (logo/wordmark, the violet gradient accent) and remain legible in common email clients (table-based HTML, inline styles, no reliance on web fonts).

## 4. Non-functional requirements

- **NFR-1 Performance:** on-demand report generation for a typical year of data shall return in well under ~2s; heavy work (PDF render) may be async with a "preparing…" state.
- **NFR-2 Privacy/PII:** outbound email/SMS and stored report artifacts shall contain only aggregates and masked identifiers — never full account numbers, credentials, or raw provider data. Aligns with the security review (L1, I4).
- **NFR-3 Security:** report deep links shall require authentication; any tokenized "view report" links shall be scoped, expiring, and per-user. Report data is always scoped by the authenticated `user_id`.
- **NFR-4 Retention:** define a retention policy for generated report artifacts and delivery logs (tie into the audit/retention policy noted in security finding I4).
- **NFR-5 Reliability:** scheduled deliveries shall be idempotent, retried on transient failure, and logged (delivery status per channel) for support/debugging — logging messages/correlation ids only, not content.
- **NFR-6 Localization:** all money, dates, and times follow user preferences; copy is centralized to allow future localization.
- **NFR-7 Deliverability:** email via an authenticated domain (SPF/DKIM/DMARC); SMS via a registered 10DLC/short code with consent records retained.

## 5. Proposed data model & API (for the build phase, not now)

- `GET /api/reports/:cadence?period=<id>` → generated report JSON (KPIs, categories, merchants, trend, insights, comparison). Built from `summarize`/`advise`.
- `GET /api/reports/:cadence/:period.pdf` and `.csv` → exports.
- `GET /api/reports/history` → past reports/deliveries for the user.
- `PUT /api/reports/preferences` → per-cadence channel + schedule settings.
- `POST /api/reports/:cadence/send` → on-demand send via chosen channel(s).
- Storage: a `report_subscriptions` table (user_id, cadence, channels, schedule, enabled), a `report_deliveries` log (user_id, cadence, period, channel, status, sent_at, dedupe_key), and optionally cached report artifacts. Phone verification state lives on the user/profile (ties into the Profile "security information" recovery phone).
- New request bodies validated with **zod** (the I3 pattern already in `server/src/validate.ts`).

## 6. Dependencies

- **Email provider** (e.g., a transactional email API) with templating + attachments and authenticated sending domain.
- **SMS provider** (e.g., Twilio) with a registered 10DLC number, STOP/HELP handling, and consent logging — gated behind the "SMS available" flag.
- **PDF renderer** (HTML-to-PDF) for report exports/attachments.
- **Scheduled-task runner** for recurring generation/delivery.
- Existing: analytics engine, preferences, category colors, auth/scoping.

---

## 7. Phased delivery

1. **Phase 1 — In-app reports.** Reports page with the four cadences, period stepping, KPIs + deltas, category/merchant/trend/insights, loading/empty states, PDF/CSV download. All from existing analytics; no external providers.
2. **Phase 2 — Email + scheduling.** HTML email templates, delivery settings, scheduled recurring sends, report history, unsubscribe/manage links.
3. **Phase 3 — SMS.** Phone verification, opt-in + consent, short digests, STOP/HELP, quiet hours — flips on the controls shipped (disabled) in Phase 1.
4. **Phase 4 — Year-in-review polish & sharing.** Richer hero layout, narrative generation, shareable export.

---

## 8. Acceptance criteria (Phase 1)

- A user can open Reports, switch between Weekly / Monthly / 6 Months / Year in Review, and step to prior periods.
- Each report shows headline KPIs with correct deltas vs. the prior comparable period, category and merchant breakdowns, a trend chart, and advisor insights — all matching the numbers the Dashboard/Advisor already produce.
- Money/dates honor the user's preferences.
- PDF and CSV downloads produce the same figures as the screen.
- Loading, empty (insufficient data), and error states render cleanly; everything respects reduce-motion and is responsive + keyboard-accessible.
- "Email me this" and "Text me this" controls are present; SMS is visibly disabled with "coming soon" until Phase 3.

## 9. Open questions

- Six-month window: rolling 6 calendar months vs. two fixed quarters?
- Year in review: calendar year only, or also a rolling 12-month version for mid-year?
- Week start day — fixed Monday, or user-configurable (add to preferences)?
- Should reports be generated live each time, or snapshotted at period close (so historical reports never change if a user re-categorizes later)? Recommendation: snapshot scheduled reports, regenerate on-demand ones live.
- Which email and SMS providers, and what is the consent-capture UX for SMS?
